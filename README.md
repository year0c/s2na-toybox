# Sonic 2 Nick Arcade Disassembly
A disassembly of the Nick Arcade prototype of Sonic the Hedgehog 2 for the Genesis/Mega Drive. Originally dumped and released on [November 7, 2006](https://forums.sonicretro.org/threads/sonic-2-early-prototype-dumped.8345/) by drx on Hidden Palace after a community fund, this build is one of the earliest avaliable, and is quite unfinished, having many leftovers from Sonic 1 (and [many other interesting things](https://tcrf.net/Proto:Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2_(Genesis)/Nick_Arcade_Prototype)) and only four of the levels, one of which was scrapped.

## Date of Prototype
While the exact date is not known, comparing what we have with events and [development calendars from Tom Payne](https://info.sonicretro.org/File:TomPaynePapers_8.5x11_Miscellaneous_Loose_Pages_(No_Order)_image1453.jpg), it's most likely that this prototype might be from **late April of 1992**.

## About this Prototype
This prototype was once referred to simply as the "**early**" prototype of Sonic 2, before being renamed to **Nick Arcade** because of the identical similarities between this build and the one shown at Nickelodeon's "**Nick Arcade**" series, specifically the build shown on the second season. Another very similar prototype to this build was the one shown at the **Summer Consumer Electronics Show** held from [May 21 to May 24 of 1992 in Chicago, Illinois](https://segaretro.org/index.php?title=File:SegaPro_UK_09.pdf&page=18). [The cartridge](https://hiddenpalace.org/File:S2b_cart_case.jpg) that drx obtained came with a holographic label of Sonic and Tails in their American/European artwork with a 2 in the centre of the label with multiple Sega logos as the backdrop. They dropped the idea of holographic cartridge labels soon after producing a few rolls of them due to [the cost of producing them](https://x.com/AlNilsen/status/483999961458016256). This cartridge is the only one known to exist with this holographic label intact.

## How the Prototype was obtained
In late October of 2006, drx made contact with an anonymous individual from a press that said they had a prototype of Sonic the Hedgehog 2. Anon set the price for the prototype at 1,500 USD, which drx quickly followed with a community fundraiser to obtain the prototype. Originally expecting it to be a later prototype than that of the Simon Wai prototype, it turned out to be one of the earliest, which he immediately dumped and uploaded onto Hidden Palace on November 7, 2006.
